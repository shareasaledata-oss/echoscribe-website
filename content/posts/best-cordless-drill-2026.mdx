A great cordless drill is the cornerstone of any toolkit. Whether you are hanging shelves, assembling furniture, or framing a wall, the right drill makes every task faster, more precise, and less fatiguing. After three months of testing, here is our definitive ranking.

## What We Tested

We evaluated each drill across five criteria: **maximum torque**, **battery runtime**, **weight and ergonomics**, **clutch sensitivity**, and **value for money**. Each drill drove 200 screws into softwood and hardwood, bored 50 holes in steel, and mixed two batches of mortar.

## Top Pick: Milwaukee M18 FUEL

The Milwaukee M18 FUEL remains the gold standard for professional users. Its brushless motor delivers 1,200 in-lbs of torque — more than most users will ever need — and the RedLithium battery maintains consistent power even as it depletes.

**Pros:** Exceptional power, longest runtime in class, superior build quality  
**Cons:** Premium price, heavier than compact alternatives

## Runner-Up: DeWalt DCD800

DeWalt's compact flagship is the pick for users who prioritize comfort over raw power. At 2.2 lbs, it is the lightest full-featured drill we tested, and its 60 clutch positions give fine-grained control for delicate applications.

> "The best drill is not the most powerful one — it is the one you will actually reach for."

## Budget Winner: Ryobi PCL206K2

For occasional use and light projects, Ryobi's 18V ONE+ platform offers compelling value. The PCL206K2 may lack the torque of premium options, but its 24-position clutch and included carry bag make it an excellent starter set.

## Battery Platform Considerations

Before choosing a drill, consider your existing battery ecosystem. Committing to a single platform (Milwaukee, DeWalt, Makita, or Ryobi) allows you to share batteries across all your tools — a significant long-term cost saving.

## Our Recommendation

For most homeowners: the **DeWalt DCD800**. For professionals or serious DIYers who need maximum capability: the **Milwaukee M18 FUEL**. For budget-conscious beginners: the **Ryobi PCL206K2**.
