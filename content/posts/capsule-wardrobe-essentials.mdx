The average piece of fast fashion is worn seven times before being discarded. A well-chosen capsule item, by contrast, can serve you for a decade. The economics of quality are compelling: a $300 coat worn for ten years costs less per wear than a $50 coat replaced annually.

## The Philosophy

A capsule wardrobe is defined by versatility and longevity. Every piece should work with at least three others in the collection, suit multiple occasions with minimal adjustment, and be constructed from materials that improve with age rather than deteriorating.

## The Essential 10

**1. Crisp White Shirt**  
The white shirt is the most versatile garment ever created. Tucked with tailored trousers, open over a t-shirt, or under a blazer — it adapts completely. Look for: poplin or broadcloth, mother-of-pearl buttons, reinforced cuffs.

**2. Dark Straight-Leg Jeans**  
A dark indigo rinse reads as smart-casual in almost any context. Look for: 98-100% cotton (stretch versions relax and bag at the knees over time), a mid-rise cut, a clean hem with minimal distressing.

**3. Tailored Trousers**  
In charcoal, navy, or camel. Wool-blend options work across seasons. The fit at the hip and thigh determines how good trousers look — alterations are worth the investment.

**4. Quality White Tee**  
The foundation of everything. Pima or Egyptian cotton, substantial weight (180gsm minimum), cut that skims rather than clings.

**5. Cashmere or Merino Crew Neck**  
In a neutral that works with your palette. Mongolian or Scottish cashmere — not blended. Hand wash cold and it will outlast synthetic alternatives by decades.

> "Style is not about following trends. It is about knowing what suits you and wearing it with conviction."

**6. Tailored Blazer**  
A navy or charcoal blazer transforms almost any outfit. Lightly structured, with natural shoulder construction, in a wool or wool-blend fabric.

**7. Classic Trench Coat**  
Double-breasted, khaki or camel, mid-calf length. The trench works with everything from jeans to evening wear.

**8. Quality Leather Belt**  
Often overlooked, a quality leather belt with a simple buckle ties a formal outfit together. Full-grain leather, not bonded. It should darken and patina with wear.

**9. Ankle Boots or Derby Shoes**  
One pair of quality leather footwear that bridges smart and casual: clean white leather sneakers or simple leather ankle boots in black or cognac.

**10. Structured Bag**  
A leather tote or structured shoulder bag in a neutral that carries everything you need. This is the piece to invest most heavily in — it is seen in every context.
